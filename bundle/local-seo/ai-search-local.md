AI assistants and AI Overviews are now a real local-discovery surface. Google's own guidance is that AI features run on the same systems as ordinary Search — there's no separate "AI SEO" trick, and normal indexing/eligibility is the floor. So the work is mostly good SEO done well, plus attention to where AI gets its facts.

# What the data suggests

* AI traffic tends to **convert well**: Seer Interactive's case study saw ChatGPT traffic convert at ~15.9% vs ~1.76% for Google organic — though that's a single client, so treat it as directional, not a benchmark.
* **Brand mentions** matter more than backlinks for AI visibility (Ahrefs) — see [local links & authority](/local-seo/local-authority-links.md).
* AI assistants generally pull from the open web and major third-party sources rather than reading Google Business Profile directly, so your presence on independent sites and directories ([NAP & citations](/local-seo/nap-citations.md)) does real work.

# What to do

1. Get the SEO fundamentals right — indexable, eligible, well-structured pages.
2. Build genuine brand mentions and earned media.
3. Be present and consistent on the third-party sources AI tools commonly cite (review sites, directories, community platforms).
4. Claim under-used surfaces like Bing Places and Apple Business Connect.
