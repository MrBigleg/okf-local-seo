Your Google Business Profile is the single biggest lever on local pack visibility (the top-three map results), per Whitespark's ranking-factors study. We don't quote a precise weighting — Whitespark's exact percentages aren't published — but its relative ranking is consistent and well-established.

# What to check

* Primary category — the most important setting on the profile. Pick the most specific category that genuinely fits; a wrong primary category actively suppresses visibility.
* Relevant secondary categories that reflect real services.
* A complete, accurate profile: name, address (if applicable), hours, phone, website.
* Photos and regular activity (posts, updates) — an actively maintained profile reads as a live business.
* Reviews flowing steadily — see [reviews & reputation](/local-seo/reviews-reputation.md).
* The website URL strategy — point the GBP link at a relevant, well-optimised page rather than reflexively at the homepage.

# On-page detection signals

When auditing from the website alone, look for a Maps embed, a place reference, or a reviews widget as evidence the GBP exists and is linked.

# Scoring guide

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| Full | Accurate primary category, complete profile, active posts/photos, steady reviews. |
| Partial | Profile present but incomplete or inactive. |
| Low | No visible GBP integration on the website. |
