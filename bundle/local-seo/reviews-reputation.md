Reviews are a major local ranking and conversion signal, and **recency beats raw count**. Sterling Sky's case-study research describes an **18-day rule**: rankings can start to slip once a business goes roughly two to three weeks without a new review. It's a community-recognised pattern, not confirmed Google policy — but the practical takeaway is sound: keep reviews coming in steadily.

# Rating thresholds (BrightLocal)

Consumer expectations have climbed:

* **68%** won't use a business rated under 4 stars (up from 55% the prior year).
* **31%** require at least 4.5 stars (up from 17%).

So the bar for "acceptable" has moved up roughly half a star in two years.

# What to check

* Total Google review count visible on page or in schema (`aggregateRating`).
* Star rating against the thresholds above.
* Recency — is there a steady, recent flow rather than a stale cluster?
* Owner responses, especially to negatives.
* Presence across more than one review platform.
* **No review gating** — pre-screening for happy customers before steering them to a review platform breaches Google's policies and risks FTC enforcement. Ask everyone.

# Industry notes

* Healthcare: HIPAA limits what you can say in responses — don't confirm or deny that a reviewer is a patient.
* Legal: mind attorney-client privilege in responses.

# Scoring guide

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| Full | Healthy count, 4.5+ stars, recent steady flow, owner responses, multi-platform. |
| Partial | Some reviews but gaps in recency, rating, or response rate. |
| Low | Few reviews, stale, no responses, single platform. |
