Real-time local inventory is the branch-level proof layer telling a shopper, search engine, or AI agent whether a specific product is actually available at a specific nearby location — at what price, with which pickup/reservation options. It's the retail equivalent of a structured restaurant menu: current, location-specific data that makes local commerce *actionable*, not just discoverable.

# Why it matters

Consumers often want to buy locally but bounce between Google, AI, and brand sites when inventory facts conflict. For higher-value purchases, survey data attributed to Lastmile / Greg Sterling reported that pre-visit confirmation of local availability is important to the large majority of buyers, and that real-time availability is a leading factor in choosing local over remote shipping. *(Figures to be restated with exact numbers and source on fact-check.)*

# Requirements

* Branch-specific product pages or inventory views.
* Current stock status, near real-time.
* Accurate price and promotion data by location.
* Reserve-online / pickup-in-store, or a call-to-confirm fallback.
* Nearby-location alternatives when the first choice is out of stock.
* Crawlable implementation — don't hide facts behind brittle JavaScript.
* Consistency across first-party site, Merchant Center, and [GBP](/local-seo/gbp-signals.md).

# Agentic angle

In [Agentic Commerce Readiness](/agentic/agentic-commerce-readiness.md), real-time inventory is one of the facts an agent needs before it can safely recommend, reserve, or buy. If it's inconsistent, the agent refuses, defers to a phone call, or routes the shopper elsewhere.
