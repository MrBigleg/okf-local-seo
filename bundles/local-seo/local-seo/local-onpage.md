Dedicated, genuinely useful service pages are among the strongest local on-page signals (Whitespark ranks dedicated service pages near the top of its local organic factors). This is where most local sites win or lose.

# What to check

* Title tag and H1 carry the city + service intent.
* NAP (name, address, phone) visible in the page HTML — footer, contact section, or header. See [NAP consistency & citations](/local-seo/nap-citations.md).
* One dedicated page per core service, with real, specific content.
* Click-to-call (`tel:`) and a contact form above the fold.
* Sensible internal linking — every important page reachable in a few clicks, with descriptive anchor text.

# Location pages (multi-location)

The big risk is **doorway pages** — near-identical templates with only the town name swapped. The quick test: if you can swap the city and the copy still reads fine, it's a doorway page, and Google's core updates have repeatedly hit that pattern. Make each page genuinely unique — local photos, area-specific testimonials, local FAQs — and give each its own [LocalBusiness schema](/local-seo/local-schema.md) with a unique `@id`. Prefer crawlable, server-rendered location URLs under a tidy `/locations/city/` structure.

# Scoring guide

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| Full | City in title + H1, NAP visible, dedicated service pages, no doorway patterns, clean internal linking. |
| Partial | Some local signals but missing service pages or doorway-page risk. |
| Low | Generic title/H1, NAP not visible, thin location pages. |
