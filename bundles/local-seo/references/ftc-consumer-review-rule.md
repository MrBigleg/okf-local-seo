The FTC's Rule on the Use of Consumer Reviews and Testimonials and the Commission's own Q&A guidance. Primary source for [review gating](/gbp/review-gating.md) and the compliance boundaries in [reviews on GBP](/gbp/reviews.md).

# What the source establishes

* The rule took effect **21 October 2024** and authorises civil penalties for knowing violations; the FTC's December 2025 warning letters state exposure of up to **$53,088 per violation**.
* Key sections: §465.2 fake/false reviews; §465.4 incentives conditioned on sentiment; §465.5 undisclosed insider reviews; §465.6 company-controlled review entities misrepresented as independent; §465.7 review suppression; §465.8 fake indicators of social-media influence.
* On gating, verbatim from the Q&A: "Can my business ask for reviews only from customers whom we think are happy with our services? The rule does not contain a specific prohibition against such conduct. But this practice could violate the FTC Act." (citing Endorsement Guides 16 CFR 255.2(d) and (e)(11)).
* Generalised solicitations to all purchasers are expressly exempt; contacting negative reviewers to resolve issues, and asking satisfied customers to update reviews, are permitted.
* On 22 December 2025 FTC staff sent warning letters to ten companies about potential rule violations — warnings, not adjudications.
